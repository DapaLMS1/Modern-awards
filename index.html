You Deserve to be Paid Correctly!

If you're new to the workforce (especially in the crucial health and community services sector) understanding how your pay and conditions are set is non-negotiable.

In Australia, we have a system called Modern Awards. These are official legal documents that act as a safety net, guaranteeing the minimum standards for key aspects of your job, including:

  • Minimum pay rates (your legal floor)
  • Hours of work (how long you can be asked to work)
  • Breaks (rest and meal entitlements)
  • Overtime and penalties (extra pay for evenings, weekends, or excess hours)
  • Allowances (e.g., uniforms or travel costs)

Modern Award: Your Industrial Rulebook

Think of a Modern Award as the foundational rulebook for your specific job or industry. It exists to ensure fairness and prevent any employer from paying below the legal minimum.

Modern Award: The Specifics

Covers minimum pay, classification, allowances, and penalty rates (e.g., weekend rates) for specific job roles.

It's tailored to your industry.

NES: The Non-Negotiables

The National Employment Standards (NES) provide basic rights for ALL workers in Australia, like paid annual leave, sick leave, and public holidays.

It's for everyone.

The Modern Award and the NES work together to form the rock-solid foundation of fair work in Australia. You get the minimum basics (NES) plus the industry-specific details (Modern Award).

Finding Your Award

If you work in our sector, your role will be covered by a specific Modern Award. This document tells you exactly where you sit in the system. For example:

The Health Professionals and Support Services Award 2020

Commonly covers roles like: Dental Assistants, Medical Receptionists, Therapy Support Workers, and administrative roles in clinics/hospitals.

Your specific Award outlines your job classification level, which is then used to determine the minimum pay rate appropriate for your experience.
